【image_prompt】

Write ONE highly-focused, detailed English paragraph describing the entire frame, to be used directly as a prompt for AI image generation models (like FLUX/Stable Diffusion).

IMPORTANT: 
- The output MUST be entirely in English (translate all Vietnamese input data like location descriptions, character appearances, and actions into natural English).
- Do NOT use raw character names. Instead, always replace them with visual descriptive terms derived from character.description (physical traits, clothing, hair — exactly as stored, do NOT invent or substitute).
- This is natural language text, NOT JSON. No { } [ ], no field:value format.

Structure the paragraph exactly in this sequence, separated by commas:
1. Shot type & Camera Angle: Describe the frame setup (e.g., "Medium shot, eye-level angle, static frame").
2. Main Subject(s), Poses, and Actions: 
   - Carefully analyze the story flow (from current.description) and the active characters listed in current.characters[].
   - Dynamically allocate distinct positions (e.g., left, center, right, foreground, background) for each character based on their interactions, dialogue, or importance in this specific shot.
   - If there is ONLY ONE character: Describe their appearance, clothing, posture, and action naturally as the single focus of the frame.
   - If there are MULTIPLE characters (2, 3, or more): You MUST enforce strict spatial division to avoid twins/cloning and clothes-swapping (attribute leakage).
     Use the explicit prefix: "[N] distinct characters with completely different outfits and features, standing separately with clear space between them:".
     Followed immediately by describing each character in order of their allocated position, separated by commas (e.g., for 2 characters: "On the left of the frame, [describe character A's clothing, hair, pose, expression], while on the right of the frame, [describe character B's clothing, hair, pose, expression]").
     For 3 characters, use: "On the far left of the frame, [describe character A]... standing in the exact center of the frame, [describe character B]... on the far right of the frame, [describe character C]...".
     Ensure no two characters share similar clothing colors, styles, or postures.
3. Background & Environment: Detail the setting, time of day, and key visible props (from location description).
4. Lighting & Color: Specify lighting direction and quality, color tone, and depth of field (from photographyRules, e.g., "soft side lighting, warm golden color tone, shallow depth of field").
5. Art Style: Apply the top-level style parameter directly (from style, e.g., "Japanese anime art style, clean line art, vibrant colors").
6. ⚠️ Face & Skin Quality Modifiers: Append face-focused quality tags to ensure the character's face renders beautifully. Choose from the list below based on the character's personality and scene lighting (include at least 3-4 tags):
   - Skin texture: "smooth radiant skin texture", "translucent rosy skin", "delicate soft skin with subtle glow", "flawless clear complexion"
   - Facial detail: "sharply defined facial features", "perfect symmetrical face", "refined elegant facial structure"
   - Eyes: "clear sparkling eyes with natural highlights", "deep expressive eyes" (only if eyes are fully visible per shot type)
   - Lighting effect: "soft natural makeup", "gentle diffused lighting on face", "natural skin undertones with warm glow"
   - Quality: "cinematic portrait quality", "ultra high detail face rendering"
   ⚠️ IMPORTANT: Do NOT overuse. Pick 3-4 modifiers that fit the scene lighting and character's beauty archetype. Do NOT add quality tags that contradict the scene's mood (e.g., do not add "soft natural makeup" to a battle scene). If the character is in extreme close-up (face visible), use more facial detail tags. If the character is at a distance or back is visible, use fewer or none.

Requirements:
- Describe ONLY what is VISIBLE in the frame. Do NOT include audio, off-screen sounds, dialogue, text, subtitles, or speech.
- Ensure all details are translated into natural, fluent English.
- ⚠️ NO camera movement language: Do NOT include any camera movement vocabulary (e.g. push-in, track, zoom, pan, orbit, handheld, sway) in the shot composition description. Use only static framing terms: static frame, fixed frame, fixed shot, static camera.
- ⚠️ "Freeze" dynamic actions: The `description` field of the panel often contains highly dynamic actions (e.g., "pushes open the door", "stands up and walks"). In `image_prompt`, you MUST convert these into a **perfectly static, frozen posture/pose** representing the exact starting moment (T=0) — e.g. "paused mid-stride in the doorway", "hand resting on the table as she begins to rise", or "eyes fixed forward with mouth slightly open". Avoid active dynamic verbs.

【IMAGE → VIDEO CONTINUITY — MANDATORY】

The generated image_prompt describes Frame 0 of the video — the exact visual state at the moment the video begins (T = 0).

The video_prompt Action lines MUST describe what happens NEXT (T > 0), after the state shown in image_prompt.
NEVER repeat, restart, or re-describe any action or dynamic verb that is already shown as a completed or in-progress state in image_prompt.
Treat visible poses, facial expressions, gaze directions, and body states in image_prompt as already established. Do not recreate or reinforce them in video_prompt.

⚠️ Action Splitting Logic (Prevent Double Motion):
- If the storyboard `description` contains a highly dynamic action like "walks toward the table and sits down", you MUST split it cleanly:
  - `image_prompt` (T = 0) represents the starting/frozen moment: "standing near the chair, body slightly turned toward the table".
  - `video_prompt` (T > 0) represents the continuation motion: "Action: takes a slow step forward and sits down on the chair".
- Never use the same verb in both fields. If `image_prompt` has "stands up", the `video_prompt` Action MUST only describe what happens *after* standing up (e.g. "takes a step forward", "tilts head").

Examples:
  ❌ image_prompt: "opens the door"      → video Action: "opens the door"
  ❌ image_prompt: "exhales a deep sigh" → video Action: "exhales a deep sigh"
  ❌ image_prompt: "stands up"           → video Action: "stands up"
  ❌ image_prompt: "eyes widened"        → video Action: "eyes widen"
  ❌ image_prompt: "walking into room"   → video Action: "walking into room"

  ✅ image_prompt: "opens the door"      → video Action: "pauses in the doorway, gaze slowly sweeping inside"
  ✅ image_prompt: "exhales a deep sigh" → video Action: "rubs his forehead gently, eyes drifting downward"
  ✅ image_prompt: "stands up"           → video Action: "takes a slow step forward, shoulders settling"
  ✅ image_prompt: "eyes widened"        → video Action: "holds gaze fixed, a faint tremble in the lower lip"
  ✅ image_prompt: "walking into room"   → video Action: "slows to a stop, head turning to survey the room"

Treat image_prompt as the first frame already rendered.
Video actions must begin after that frame — NEVER reproduce it.

【video_prompt】

Each panel has an existingVideoPrompt from Phase 3 (may be empty).
Task: CONSTRAIN camera and background to static. Allow moderate character actions with slow-motion.

Output format — follow this EXACT structure. Each panel's video_prompt may contain one or more Shot blocks based on duration:

```
[Scene] <environment description - static, no ambient motion>
[Characters] <ALWAYS include this section. Filter description by shot type per rule 1.>
<character 1 name>: <FULL description (Full/Wide) or UPPER BODY only (Medium) per rule 1 + emotional state in ≤5 words>. <character 2 name>: <same>.

Shot 1 (<shotType>, <duration_seconds>s): Static camera, character actions allowed
<Character 1>: "<dialogue from srtSegment (only if present, max 10 words)>"
Action: <specific character micro-actions, speed/fluidity, emotional nuances>
```

Number of Shot blocks per video_prompt is determined by duration:
- duration < 4s  → 1 shot
- duration 4-5s  → 2 shots (same framing and camera angle, split motion progression only — do NOT change angle between shots)
- duration 6-8s  → 3 shots
- duration > 8s  → 4+ shots

Number Shot starting from 1 within each video_prompt entry.

Key rules:
1. [Characters] section is CONDITIONAL but NEVER fully omitted — ALWAYS include character names and a filtered description matching ONLY the body parts visible in the shot. Full character.description is the source; prune it to what the frame actually shows. This prevents the model from generating body parts not present in the input image.
   - **Full Shot / Long Shot / Wide Shot** (whole body visible): FULL character.description — face, hair, outfit from head to toe, shoes. All text matches pixels.
   - **Medium Shot** (waist up visible): UPPER BODY only — face, hair, upper clothing (top, tie, jacket). Do NOT describe trousers, skirt, shoes.
   - **Close-Up / Over-the-Shoulder** (face & neck visible): HEAD+NECK only — face shape, eyes, brows, nose, lips, hair, visible neck/collar clothing. Do NOT describe torso, full outfit, lower body.
   - **Extreme Close-Up / Detail Shot** (only one body part visible): THAT PART ONLY.
     - Hand visible → describe hand: fingers, nails, cuff/sleeve end. Nothing else.
     - Eye visible → describe eye: iris, lashes, skin around eye. Nothing else.
     - Lips visible → describe lips: shape, texture. Nothing else.
   - Always append current emotional state in ≤5 words from current.characters[].acting and srtSegment context. Do NOT invent.
2. After [Characters] definition, use only the character's NAME in the Shot blocks (Dialogue/Action lines) — the model already knows who they are.
3. The [Scene] section MUST include:
   a. Full environment from current.description and location data — do not summarize.
   b. Environment MUST be described as STATIC — NO ambient motion elements (no leaves rustling, no clouds drifting, no sunlight shifting, no curtain swaying). Background must remain completely still throughout the video.
4. Shot block structure (MANDATORY MULTI-LINE FORMAT): Each Shot must be structured as a multi-line block:
   - Line 1 (Camera & Environment): `Shot X (<shotType>, <duration>s): Static camera` (e.g., "Shot 1 (Medium Shot, 3s): Static camera, environment remains unchanged.")
   - Line 2 (Dialogue - ONLY if srtSegment is present): `<Character Name>: "<short dialogue phrase>"` (translate dialogue to English if input is in Vietnamese/Chinese, keep to ≤10 words)
   - Line 3 (or Line 2 if no dialogue - Action): `Action: <specific character micro-actions, speed/fluidity, emotional nuances>` (e.g., "Action: Bạch Dương pushes the door open and steps into the room, his gaze sweeping forward in a slow, deliberate motion.")
5. Character Actions & Spatial Alignment: Each Action line's action MUST respect spatial positions from image_prompt. If a character is on the left, their action starts from that position.
6. Dialogue in Shot blocks: ALLOWED only when srtSegment is non-empty AND the action involves speaking. Keep to ≤10 words, written exactly on its own line: `Character Name: "Dialogue text"`. Purpose is lip-sync anchor only. Do NOT fabricate dialogue not present in srtSegment. If srtSegment is empty, omit dialogue completely.
7. If existingVideoPrompt exists, enrich it to match this format — add missing [Scene] and [Characters] sections with full detail, add species info, split into correct number of Shot blocks based on duration
8. View Boundary & Camera Scale Constraint (CRITICAL FOR CONTINUITY): Video generation models generate motion using the generated static image as the absolute source.
   - You MUST NEVER zoom out or transition from a tight/close-up/detail shot to a wider shot (e.g., transitioning from a Close-Up of a hand in Shot 1 to a Wide Shot of the room in Shot 2). AI video models cannot maintain facial or clothing consistency when "outpainting" or hallucinating areas not present in the input image.
   - If current.shotType is a Close-Up, Extreme Close-Up, or Detail Shot (e.g., close-up of a prop, hands, or eyes), ALL Shot blocks in the video_prompt must remain strictly locked within that close-up boundary.
   - For close-up panels, only allow: static camera, slow panning/tracking within the close-up, or micro-movements (e.g., fingers twitching, eyes blinking, lips quivering). Do NOT attempt to reveal the face, body, or wider background if they are not visible in the close-up image.
9. Action Visibility Constraint (MANDATORY): Action lines MUST NOT describe or imply any body part, feature, or motion that is NOT visible in the input image. Violating this forces the model to recompose the frame to show the referenced body part, causing scene drift:
   - Hand visible only   → only hand movements (fingers curling, tightening, holding). NO gaze, face, expressions.
   - Eye visible only    → only eye movements (blinking, pupil shifts). NO hand, head rotation.
   - Face visible        → facial expressions, eyes, lips, head movement.
   - Full body visible   → full body movements, posture changes, walking.
   - If an action requires showing a body part outside the frame, DO NOT write that action. Drop it or reframe.

⚠️ **Anti-Jitter / Speed Control Rules** (violation causes stutter, distortion):
- LIMIT ACTIONS per Shot: < 4s → 1 primary action only (a secondary micro-expression is optional but not required). 4-5s → max 2 actions. Never chain 3+ actions in one shot — the AI will rush through them causing jitter
- Keep actions simple and slow: "slowly tilts head", "gently raises hand", "subtle smile" — avoid rapid sequences
- ❌ Bad: "Sonic looks up, waves enthusiastically, then runs away" (too many actions chained = jitter)
- ✅ Good: "Slow-motion. Sonic in a gentle, smooth motion slowly tilts his head upward with a subtle smile. Smooth and fluid movement throughout"
- Use speed keywords in every Shot: "slow-motion", "smooth and fluid", "gentle", "subtle"
- Camera movement: "static camera", "no camera movement" — NEVER use "push", "dolly", "track", "pan", "zoom"

If existingVideoPrompt is NOT empty:
  1. COMPARE Action lines against image_prompt — remove or replace any action that duplicates a motion or state already visible in image_prompt (apply IMAGE → VIDEO CONTINUITY rule above)
  2. RE-VERIFY Shot block count matches the duration rule above — add or remove Shot blocks if needed
  3. Preserve narrative meaning while rewriting actions to satisfy all constraints — do NOT preserve exact actions verbatim
  4. RESTRUCTURE to match the format above if it doesn't already
  5. ENRICH: focus purely on character micro-actions (NO camera movements allowed). Apply rule 1 to filter [Characters] section by shot type — prune full character.description to visible parts only. Trim to upper body for Medium, head/neck for Close-Up, the specific visible part for ECU/Detail.
  6. ABSOLUTELY DO NOT re-describe the static scene already in image_prompt
  7. Enforce static camera: Ensure all camera parameters are set to "static camera", "camera remains fixed".

If existingVideoPrompt IS EMPTY → generate from scratch:
  - duration < 4s: micro-motion only (blink, breathe, lip quiver, subtle gaze shift) — 1 Shot block, keep format concise
  - duration 4-5s: moderate motion (slow head turn, gentle gesture, breathing) — 2 Shot blocks, NO camera movement
  - duration ≥ 6s: moderate motion (standing up, walking slowly, turning body) — 3+ Shot blocks, NO camera movement, keep slow-motion
  - duration null: subtle motion
   - Always use the EXACT format: [Scene] + [Characters] + Shot

【LTX Video 2.3 Specific Guidance】

This pipeline uses LTX Video 2.3 with IC-LoRA (Identity Consistency LoRA) and CropGuides for enhanced character consistency and reduced outpainting.

What LTX 2.3 + IC-LoRA FIXES:
✅ Character face drift (IC-LoRA strength 0.9 maintains identity)
✅ Attribute leakage between characters (IC-LoRA Dual-Character)
✅ Frame expansion (CropGuides crop to visible region)

What LTX 2.3 + IC-LoRA DOES NOT FIX:
❌ Camera push/dolly/track → still causes outpainting at frame edges
❌ Background changes (sunlight shifting, leaves rustling) → still redraws background latent
❌ Character actions requiring invented pixels → still outpaints

Therefore:
- Camera: MUST remain static (NO push, dolly, track, pan, zoom)
- Background: MUST remain static (NO ambient motion elements)
- Character actions: ALLOWED and can be moderate (head turns, gestures, standing up, walking) thanks to IC-LoRA, but MUST use slow-motion and smooth transitions
- Multi-character scenes: Lower risk due to IC-LoRA Dual-Character, but still enforce spatial separation

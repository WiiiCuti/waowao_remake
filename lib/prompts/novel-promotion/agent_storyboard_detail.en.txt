【Shot Language Library】

**Shot Sizes**:
- Extreme Long Shot: grandiose scenery, epic feel, tiny figures
- Long Shot / Full Shot: establishing environment, character relationships
- Medium Shot: dialogue, interaction, daily life
- Close-Up: emotions, reactions
- Extreme Close-Up: eyes, hands, key props
- Macro Close-Up: pupils, lips, a single teardrop, etc.

**Camera Angles**:
- Eye-Level: daily, equal, natural
- Low Angle: sense of oppression, nobility
- High Angle: sense of insignificance, overview
- Over-the-Shoulder: dialogue, confrontation
- Dutch Angle: unease, tension
- POV (Point of View): sense of immersion

【「Select Shot Style Based on scene_type (Archetype)」

⚠️ `scene_type` is now one of 10 Cinematic Archetypes set by Phase 1. Use the archetype to guide
your choice of shot_type and camera_move for each panel.

**Impact** (decisive action):
- Shot sequence: Full/Wide (setup) → Medium (decisive moment) → Close-Up (aftermath)
- Dutch angle and Low Angle allowed at the decisive moment to amplify impact
- ECU on the point of contact or key prop is encouraged

**Duel** (back-and-forth contest):
- Alternate Low-Angle and Eye-Level between the two combatants/opponents
- No single character may have Low-Angle advantage 2 consecutive panels in a row
- Intercut Close-Ups of reactions and decisive moments; use Wide only at start/end

**Pursuit** (chase / escape):
- Wide Shot when distance is large; Medium when distance is closing
- Handheld or Stabilized Tracking to convey urgency
- Shallow DOF on the pursuer or pursued to blur background

**Journey** (travel / exploration):
- Tracking, follow, or aerial perspective; character moves through space
- Wide Establishing at start; gradually tighten to Medium as narrative focus grows

**Atmosphere** (mood / emotional texture):
- Prefer Close-Up and Extreme Close-Up to capture micro-expressions
- Static camera only; no handheld shake
- Minimal panel count — sustained framing creates emotional weight

**Reveal** (discovery / surprise):
- Begin with tight or obstructed framing (OTS, partial, Dutch angle)
- Progress to wider, cleaner framing as the reveal unfolds
- Use High Angle before reveal, Eye-Level or Low Angle after to shift power

**Confrontation** (tense dialogue / conflict):
- Default to OTS (Over-the-Shoulder) Medium Shot
- If power shifts, cross the 180° axis in the next panel to signal the change
- Intercut with Close-Up on face during key emotional beats

**Interrogation** (asymmetric power):
- Questioner: Low Angle (dominant)
- Subject: High Angle (submissive, under pressure)
- Silent moments: push-in to Extreme Close-Up on the subject's face or hands

**Negotiation** (equal-power dialogue):
- Symmetrical Medium Two-Shot as default
- Alternate Eye-Level OTS shots of both characters at matched framing sizes
- No Low Angle advantage for either party

**insert** (extreme detail / causal action):
- Extreme Close-Up or Macro Close-Up on a specific prop, hand, eye, or physical detail
- Static camera; extremely shallow depth of field
- Action must be highly specific micro-movement (e.g. droplet falling, fingers twitching)

【「Shot Continuity Rules」
- Shots must be continuous with no breaks
- Panels in the same group must progress step by step: Long → Medium → Close or Close → Medium → Long
  Exception 1: merged action+dialogue panels stay at Medium Shot for both action and dialogue (no progression to Close-Up)
  Exception 2: Duel archetype alternates matched shot scales (e.g., alternating Close-Ups or Mediums)
  Exception 3: Atmosphere archetype may intentionally hold static Close-Up/Extreme Close-Up framing across consecutive panels
- New scenes generally require an establishing Full Shot
- Panels should be diverse; do not repeat similar shot sizes
  Exception: consecutive merged action+dialogue panels may repeat Medium Shot, or alternate with Over-the-Shoulder Medium Shot for variety

⚠️ Double Contrast fallback: If Phase 2a (Cinematographer) has not specified a
  camera characteristic for a given archetype in context, YOU must independently
  choose a camera characteristic that avoids repeating the same shot scale AND
  camera characteristic as the immediately preceding panel. Apply the Double Contrast rule:
  at least one of (shot scale / camera characteristic) MUST differ between adjacent panels.
  Exception: Atmosphere archetype consecutive panels may share Static + Close-Up intentionally.

【video_prompt Writing Philosophy — CINEMATIC NATURALISM】

⚠️ CORE PRINCIPLE: Write each video_prompt as if you are a cinematographer describing a real scene to a camera operator. Describe what you SEE — naturally, cinematically, with life.

**Think like a real film scene:**
- A real person doesn't just "blink once and stop". They breathe, shift weight, glance around, react to their environment, feel emotions on their face.
- A real environment doesn't freeze. Rain falls. Wind moves hair and fabric. Light shifts. People in the background go about their lives.
- A real conversation isn't just "lips move slightly". People gesture, nod, react, lean in, pull back, their whole body speaks.

**Write naturally. Don't overthink it. Don't force rigid rules. Just describe the scene as it would unfold in a real film.**

**What makes a scene feel alive:**
- Characters move continuously — not in isolated actions, but in flowing, connected motion
- The environment has its own life — weather, light, background activity
- Emotions show through the whole body, not just the face
- Actions have weight and consequence — reaching for something, turning away, stepping closer
- Silence is still motion — breathing, thinking, the subtle shifts of being alive

**What kills a scene:**
- ❌ "frozen", "completely still", "perfectly static", "no motion", "motionless", "tableau"
- ❌ Describing the environment as static or frozen
- ❌ Single isolated micro-actions ("he blinks once")
- ❌ "his lips move slightly" for dialogue (describe real speaking)
- ❌ Characters standing completely still except for one tiny movement

**Camera**: Static (Image-to-Video technical constraint — the starting image is still, so camera cannot move). But everything WITHIN the frame is alive and moving.
⚠️ LTX I2V ENGINE NOTE — Camera Characteristic is FEEL, not literal movement:
When an Archetype (e.g. Pursuit or Journey) suggests "Handheld" or "Tracking", you MUST translate these into energetic CHARACTER motion or rapid ENVIRONMENTAL activity within the static frame.
"Handheld" does NOT mean you write "camera shakes" — it means you write the characters moving with urgency, or wind gusting chaotically. DO NOT write camera movement verbs (shake, pan, track) in the video_prompt. Convert all motion energy into the scene itself.

**Character Description Format**: Use full visual attributes (species, appearance, clothing, age, gender) from character data — e.g. "penguin young man in orange goggles", "hedgehog youth boy with blue spikes". Do NOT use character names alone.

【Shot Language Library】

**Shot Sizes**:
- Extreme Long Shot: grandiose scenery, epic feel, tiny figures
- Long Shot / Full Shot: establishing environment, character relationships
- Medium Shot: dialogue, interaction, daily life
- Close-Up: emotions, reactions
- Extreme Close-Up: eyes, hands, key props
- Macro Close-Up: pupils, lips, a single teardrop, etc.

**Camera Angles**:
- Eye-Level: daily, equal, natural
- Low Angle: sense of oppression, nobility (action/epic scenes)
- High Angle: sense of insignificance, overview (grand scenes)
- Over-the-Shoulder: dialogue, confrontation
- Dutch Angle: unease, tension (suspense/tense scenes)
- POV (Point of View): sense of immersion

**Camera Movements**:
- Static: gaze, silence, daily dialogue
- Slow Push-in / Slow Pull-out: emotional buildup, revelation, gentle transition
- Tracking: character movement, daily walking
- Snap Zoom In / Snap Zoom Out: shock, impact (tense scenes)
- Orbit / Crane Up / Dive Down: ritualistic feel, epic feel (grand scenes)
- Handheld Shake: chaos, tension (action scenes)

【Select Shot Style Based on scene_type】

**daily (Daily / Dialogue)**:
- Primarily Medium Shot and Close-Up with occasional Extreme Close-Up
- Primarily Eye-Level, alternating Over-the-Shoulder
- ✅ Prioritize slow push-in / slow pull-out / slight tracking, avoid purely static shots
- Camera movement terms: slowly push in, gently track, slight sway, slow orbit
- Character actions: even in dialogue scenes, add subtle movements (nod, turn head, gesture, walk)

**emotion (Emotional / Lyrical)**:
- Close-Up and Extreme Close-Up to capture emotion
- Use Macro Close-Up at emotional climax
- ✅ Prioritize slow push-in, orbiting camera, avoid purely static shots
- Camera movement terms: slowly push in, gently orbit, slight sway
- Character actions: gently raise head, turn around, lower head, raise hand to wipe tears, walk toward window

**action (Action / Combat)**:
- Rapid shot size switching, alternating Extreme Close-Up + Full Shot
- Low Angle, High Angle, Dutch Angle to increase impact
- Snap zoom in/out, tracking, handheld shake
- Camera movement terms: sudden, swift, rapid, explosive

**epic (Epic / Grand)**:
- Must use Extreme Long Shot to establish scale
- High Angle, crane up, dive down to showcase grandeur
- Place figures at frame edges to emphasize their smallness
- Camera movement terms: slowly crane up, rapidly dive down, orbit

**suspense (Suspense / Tense)**:
- POV, Dutch Angle
- Slow push-in to create pressure
- Sudden cuts to break rhythm

【Shot Continuity Rules】
- Shots must be continuous with no breaks
- Panels in the same group must progress step by step: Long → Medium → Close or Close → Medium → Long
- New scenes generally require an establishing Full Shot
- Panels should be diverse; do not repeat similar shot sizes
- Keep the frame alive, not stiff

【video_prompt Writing Rules - IMPORTANT】

Describe characters using their full visual attributes (species, appearance, clothing, age, gender) from character data — e.g. "penguin young man in orange goggles", "hedgehog youth boy with blue spikes". Do NOT use character names alone, and do NOT reduce to generic "young man" without species.
- Format: species + appearance + action + camera movement + environment
- Select dynamic intensity based on scene type
- Do not include content not present in the storyboard panel
- Movement-related shots must be dynamic; static shots must have rich body language and expression
- If the source text involves speaking, the prompt must explicitly state "speaking" or "talking"

⚠️ 【Dynamic Priority Principle - Core Rule】

The video must not be stiff! Every video_prompt must include elements of "motion":

1. **Character Action Word Bank** (must use):
   - Head: turn head, nod, raise head, lower head, tilt head, look back
   - Hands: raise hand, wave, point, clench fist, put down, pick up, touch
   - Body: walk, turn around, stand up, sit down, lean forward, step back, approach
   - Expression: brows furrow slightly, corners of mouth lift, eyes flicker, smile gently

2. **Camera Movement Word Bank** (prioritize these; avoid "static"):
   - Common: slowly push in, gently track, slight sway, orbit shot
   - Dynamic: handheld tracking, slight shake, slow orbit, crane up shot
   - Intense: rapid push in, fast tracking, sudden pull back, dive down

3. **Forbid Purely Static Descriptions**:
   ❌ Wrong: "young woman sitting on a sofa, static camera"
   ✅ Right: "young woman sits on the sofa gently turning her head, camera slowly pushes in toward her profile"

   ❌ Wrong: "middle-aged man stands at the door, stern expression"
   ✅ Right: "middle-aged man pushes the door open and walks in, brows furrowed, handheld camera follows"

4. **Even dialogue scenes must have motion**:
   ❌ Wrong: "young man speaking, static camera"
   ✅ Right: "young man speaks while gesturing with both hands, nodding gently, camera slowly pushes in"

⚠️ **Flashback / Narration / Inner Monologue Rules**:
- Forbid writing only figures standing still in contemplation, dazed, or empty shots
- video_prompt must depict the **actual actions and scenes** described in the narrative content
- Image must be strongly tied to the plot, do not just write "figure standing and reminiscing"
- Example: narration about "that fateful meeting back then" → must write the actual action scene of the meeting

**Age Group Classification** (only use these terms):
- Youth Boy / Youth Girl: approximately 10-16 years old
- Young Man / Young Woman: approximately 17-30 years old
- Middle-Aged Man / Middle-Aged Woman: approximately 31-50 years old
- Elderly Man / Elderly Woman: 50+ years old

⚠️ 【Extreme Close-Up Shots Must Use Static Camera】
- When shot type is "Extreme Close-Up" (e.g., hand close-up, object close-up, partial close-up, etc.)
- video_prompt must explicitly write "static camera" or "camera remains fixed"
- Forbid any camera movement in extreme close-up shots
- Reason: extreme close-up only shows a partial area; camera movement would expose other parts

**Examples** (note dynamic elements):
- Daily dialogue: "young woman picks up a coffee cup blowing gently, looks up toward the window, sunlight falls on her profile, camera slowly pushes in toward her silhouette"
- Action scene: "youth boy leaps into the air swinging a sword in an arc, robes billowing, handheld low-angle camera follows upward"
- Emotional scene: "young woman slowly lowers her head, teardrops slide down her cheek, raises her hand to wipe the corner of her eye, camera gently orbits around her"
- Dialogue scene: "middle-aged man taps fingers on the table, speaking with a stern expression, camera sways slightly while filming"
- Walking scene: "young man strides quickly down the street, wind blows the hem of his clothes, handheld camera follows filming"
- Extreme close-up: "a hand slowly turns the page of a book, fingertips gently trace across the text, static camera"

【Shot Language Library】

**Shot Sizes**:
- Extreme Long Shot: grandiose scenery, epic feel, tiny figures
- Long Shot / Full Shot: establishing environment, character relationships
- Medium Shot: dialogue, interaction, daily life
- Close-Up: emotions, reactions
- Extreme Close-Up: eyes, hands, key props
- Macro Close-Up: pupils, lips, a single teardrop, etc.

**Camera Angles**:
- Eye-Level: daily, equal, natural
- Low Angle: sense of oppression, nobility (action/epic scenes)
- High Angle: sense of insignificance, overview (grand scenes)
- Over-the-Shoulder: dialogue, confrontation
- Dutch Angle: unease, tension (suspense/tense scenes)
- POV (Point of View): sense of immersion

**I2V Character Motion Library (Translating Cinematic Intent to Subject Motion)**:
- Intimacy (Slow Push-In) ➔ Expression deepens, eyes lock, subtle head tilt forward, gentle smile
- Tension (Dutch Angle / Push) ➔ Posture stiffens, pupils contract, rapid shallow breathing, lips press tight
- Grandeur (Crane Up / Orbit) ➔ In Full Shot: slow head turn scanning vast space. In Close-up: eyes widen in awe, chest rises
- Action Tracking (Track / Pan) ➔ In Full Shot: character walks slowly through the frame. If not Full Shot ➔ keep camera static, character performs action within visible bounds
- Emotional Climax (Orbit Close-up) ➔ Micro-expression cycle: slow blink ➔ lips part slightly ➔ swallow ➔ gaze slowly drops
- Shock / Surprise (Snap Zoom) ➔ Sudden expression shift: eyes snap wide, body jolts slightly, breath catches
- Reveal (Slow Pull-Out) ➔ Eyes slowly open, head gradually raises, hands slowly unclench
- Urgency / Chaos (Handheld Shake) ➔ Rapid eye darts, hands tremble slightly, quick head turns, uneven shallow breathing
- Contemplation (Static Slow Push) ➔ Blink slowly, gaze becomes unfocused, chest rises and falls gently, slow swallow
- Dialogue (Shot/Reverse-Shot) ➔ Speaker: lips move naturally, subtle micro-nods. Listener: eyes blink softly, slight tilt of the head, expression shifts in reaction

【Select Shot Style Based on scene_type】

**daily (Daily / Dialogue)**:
- Primarily Medium Shot and Close-Up with occasional Extreme Close-Up
- Primarily Eye-Level, alternating Over-the-Shoulder
- ✅ Camera MUST remain static (Static camera) to ensure LTX Video compatibility
- Camera movement: static camera, fixed camera
- Character actions: add subtle micro-movements (nod, tilt head, blink, smile, gentle gaze shift)

**emotion (Emotional / Lyrical)**:
- Close-Up and Extreme Close-Up to capture emotion
- Use Macro Close-Up at emotional climax
- ✅ Camera MUST remain static (Static camera)
- Camera movement: static camera, fixed camera
- Character actions: gently raise or lower head, blink slowly, swallow, chest rising and falling gently

**action (Action / Combat)**:
- Rapid shot size switching, alternating Extreme Close-Up + Full Shot
- Low Angle, High Angle, Dutch Angle to increase impact
- Keep camera static or fixed frame; motion must be in the combatants' movements
- Camera movement: static camera, fixed camera (avoid rapid zooms/shakes unless absolutely necessary)

**epic (Epic / Grand)**:
- Must use Extreme Long Shot to establish scale
- High Angle, crane up, dive down to showcase grandeur
- Place figures at frame edges to emphasize their smallness
- Camera movement: static camera, fixed camera (let the immense environment stay completely still)

**suspense (Suspense / Tense)**:
- POV, Dutch Angle
- Static camera, tension held entirely through expressions and framing
- Sudden cuts to break rhythm

【Shot Continuity Rules】
- Shots must be continuous with no breaks
- Panels in the same group must progress step by step: Long → Medium → Close or Close → Medium → Long
- New scenes generally require an establishing Full Shot
- Panels should be diverse; do not repeat similar shot sizes
- Keep the frame alive, not stiff

【video_prompt Writing Rules - IMPORTANT】

Describe characters using their full visual attributes (species, appearance, clothing, age, gender) from character data — e.g. "penguin young man in orange goggles", "hedgehog youth boy with blue spikes". Do NOT use character names alone, and do NOT reduce to generic "young man" without species.

⚠️ 【I2V Core Motion Law — MANDATORY】

Every video_prompt MUST be designed for the Image-to-Video (I2V) pipeline. The camera and environment MUST remain locked and static. Pacing, emotion, and motion must come ENTIRELY from character performance.

1. **Motion Levels by Shot Size (Boundary constraints)**:
   - **Level 0 (Micro-motion) ➔ ECU / Macro Close-Up (Eyes, lips, fingers only)**: Only allow micro-expressions (blink, pupils contract, lip tremble, swallow, finger twitch, deep breath). ABSOLUTELY NO head turns, shoulder movement, or body posture changes.
   - **Level 1 (Subtle motion) ➔ Close-Up (Face, neck, shoulders only)**: Only allow Level 0 + head tilt, gentle nod, gaze shift, brow furrow, subtle smile. ABSOLUTELY NO hand gestures, walking, standing up, or body turns.
   - **Level 2 (Moderate motion) ➔ Medium Shot (Waist up)**: Allow Level 1 + hand gestures, body leans, shrugging shoulders, arms crossing. NO walking, running, or standing up.
   - **Level 3 (Full motion) ➔ Full / Long Shot (Whole body)**: Allow Level 2 + walking slowly, standing up, sitting down, turning around, taking a step forward.

2. **Temporal Speed & Action Chaining Law**:
   - **duration < 3s (Max 1 action)**: Only 1 micro-action or subtle movement. Use speed keyword: "slow-motion" (mandatory).
   - **duration 3-5s (Max 1 primary + 1 micro)**: 1 primary action (e.g. nod head) and 1 micro-action (e.g. eyes blinking). Speed keywords: "smooth, fluid, gentle".
   - **duration 6-8s (Max 2 primary actions)**: Maximum 2 primary actions chained. Speed keywords: "slow pace, gradual transition".
   - **duration > 8s (Max 3 primary actions)**: Maximum 3 actions. Speed keywords: "progressive build-up".

3. **Camera Constraint**:
   - Always write: "static camera" or "camera remains fixed".
   - Never use: "push", "dolly", "track", "pan", "zoom", "sway", "handheld", "shake" (unless extreme action/combat, and even then must keep background static).

4. **Enforce Static Camera with Character Motion**:
   ❌ Wrong (camera movement): "penguin young woman sits on the sofa gently turning her head, camera slowly pushes in toward her profile"
   ✅ Right (static camera, character motion): "penguin young woman sits on the sofa, gently tilts her head with a soft blink, camera remains completely static"

   ❌ Wrong (out-of-frame dynamic action): "penguin middle-aged man pushes the door open and walks in, brows furrowed, handheld camera follows"
   ✅ Right (pre-positioned start): "penguin middle-aged man stands at the doorway, brows furrowed, slowly takes a step forward into the room, static camera"

5. **Even dialogue scenes must have motion (via character, not camera)**:
   ❌ Wrong: "hedgehog young man speaks while gesturing with both hands, nodding gently, camera slowly pushes in"
   ✅ Right: "hedgehog young man speaks with a gentle nod, his eyes blinking softly with a calm expression, static camera"

⚠️ **Flashback / Narration / Inner Monologue Rules**:
- Forbid writing only figures standing still in contemplation, dazed, or empty shots
- video_prompt must depict the **actual actions and scenes** described in the narrative content
- Image must be strongly tied to the plot, do not just write "figure standing and reminiscing"
- Example: narration about "that fateful meeting back then" → must write the actual action scene of the meeting
